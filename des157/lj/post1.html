Best practices for form design

Filling forms and spending a lot of unnecessary time answering irrelevant questions is one of my biggest pet peeves. The article, "Best practices for form design" helped me understand how I can make an interactive and time-efficient form for the future. The article informs the readers about 20 strategies about different aspects of a form so that they can use these methods to make forms efficient. One of the most interesting parts for me was when the author talks about the one-column layout as I always thought multiple columns were better, but now that I think about it, a single column is way faster than multiple.

Furthermore, the article makes me wonder about all the small details I can now add to my elements to make the form simple flowing, and to the point. For example, distinguishing required and optional fields to avoid wastage of time, clearly defining labels to get appropriate input, preparing tabs in advance, and inserting them in the forms to ensure it follows the correct sequence and several others. Overall, now I am more aware of the different elements I can add to the form and make the respondants' job easier.
