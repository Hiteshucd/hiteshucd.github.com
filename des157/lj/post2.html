Overuse of Overlays: How to Avoid Misusing Lightboxes

Before reading the article, I never really paid attention to overlays and how they appear over the main page but this article has challenged me to view these in a new light. The article talks how about how one can present the overlays in the best possible way, with easy controls for the user to quickly complete the task. The writer, “Kathryn Whitenton”, strongly suggests applying “ the five W’s – Who, What, When, Where, and Why” approach which would always help determine whether the overlaying box is concise and easy to follow. I had an apple laptop for years and after reading the example I realized that the macox always has a separate page for overlay.

After reading this article, I visited a couple of sites such as Linkedin and Architectural Digest to see how these websites use overlays, whether they use lightboxes or adjust them with the main page. I feel confident after reading this article that in the future given the task of making overlays, I will be able to appropriately apply the concepts from this article in my presentation.
